Potato Early Blight

Introduction

Potato early blight is a common fungal disease that can cause significant yield losses in potato crops. It is caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, which can also infect tomatoes and other members of the Solanaceae family. Early blight can occur anywhere potatoes are grown, but it is most common in warm, humid climates.
